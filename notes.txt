sample rate --> how many samples per second so 500 means 500 samples per second and it measures the ecg signal in 500 different points in one second
amplitude --> the height of the ecg signal in millivolts

New pipeline = crop/pad to fixed length
Old pipeline = resample based on sampling rate metadata using linear (interpolation)
    So if ECG was recorded at:
        1000 Hz → converted to 5000 points
        15000 Hz → compressed to 5000 points
        2500 Hz → stretched to 5000 points

Original Harvard dataset --> each 10 seconds long, acquired at sampling rates of 250 or 500 Hz.
Pediatric dataset --> The sampling rate is 500 Hz and the record length is 5-120 seconds
CinC2021 dataset --> sampling frequency of 500 Hz


Quick rule at 500 Hz:
2,500 points = 5 sec
5,000 points = 10 sec
10,000 points = 20 sec
15,000 points = 30 sec
60,000 points = 120 sec

- The model ECGFounder trained on ECG which is mostly 10s and the pediatric dataset is 5-120s by the majority of the sample were 30s.
